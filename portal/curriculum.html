Curriculum Adaptation

Built for TZ old syllabus, adaptable to any math system

Current implementations target Tanzanian old syllabus Form 3 and Form 4, but FML architecture supports direct adaptation to any school math structure or concept set.

Current coverage

Form 3: relations/functions, algebra, statistics, variation, sequences, circles, earth geometry, accounting.

Form 4: coordinate geometry, area/perimeter, 3D figures, probability, trigonometry, vectors, matrices, linear programming.

Portable unit model

Each chapter is a portable unit: outcomes, formulas, tool schemas, sample prompts, and tests. This lets you remap topics to another syllabus without rebuilding from zero.

Concept-first mode

FML can also be arranged by concept families (functions, trigonometry, statistics, finance) rather than grade-level chapters, depending on school requirements.

Adaptation workflow

  1. Define curriculum outcomes and exam scope.
  2. Map outcomes to formula modules and missing solvers.
  3. Create chapter pages with local examples and language.
  4. Register AI tools and run quick/advanced validation tests.
  5. Deploy as a new route in the same portal or a standalone app.

Examples of adaptable targets

  • National secondary school math syllabi
  • IB, Cambridge, or regional exam boards
  • University bridge/prep math tracks
  • Topic-only programs (statistics-only, algebra-only)
